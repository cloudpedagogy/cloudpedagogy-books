# Generative AI Ethics

**Status:** Draft (content complete, formatting and release preparation in progress)

## Overview

*Generative AI Ethics* is a foundational, open-access book examining the ethical, social, and governance challenges introduced by generative artificial intelligence systems such as large language models, image generators, and multimodal AI tools.

The book provides a structured, accessible, and critical exploration of how generative AI reshapes truth, creativity, labour, education, governance, and human agency. It is written for educators, policymakers, researchers, professionals, and institutions seeking to engage with generative AI responsibly and thoughtfully.

This book forms part of the **CloudPedagogy** ecosystem of open educational and governance-oriented resources on AI capability, ethics, and human–AI decision-making.

---

## Scope and Themes

The book covers:

- What makes generative AI ethically distinct from earlier forms of AI
- Misinformation, truth, and epistemic risk
- Bias, fairness, and representation
- Privacy, data ownership, and consent
- Labour, creativity, and the future of work
- Human autonomy and agency
- Policy, regulation, and institutional responsibility
- Global and cross-cultural perspectives
- Ethical system design and education
- Futures of generative AI ethics and shared responsibility
